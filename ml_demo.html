The course

Real iQFoil shape: start sequence (compressed to 18s here — real racing uses 3 minutes), line closed until the gun, upwind beat, leeward gate with a free left/right choice, second beat, reach to Mark 3, half-wind finish. Marks must be properly rounded — enough sweep around the buoy in the correct direction (port at Mark 1) — and every race sails through wind shifts up to ±17° plus roaming gusts and lulls.

The learning

Every weight starts as pure Gaussian noise — no pretraining, no imitation. Each board is a 7 → 8 → 1 net (73 weights) reading mark bearing, wind angle, countdown, and leg number — output steers. An evolution strategy trains them from zero: race all 1,000, keep the top 10%, mutate, repeat. No gradients, no loss.backward().

The start

Boats must work the line during the countdown: the score at the gun rewards being at speed (not parked), sitting close under the line, and being at the favored end — the more-upwind pin or boat end, which glows orange and swaps as the wind shifts.

What emerges

Tacking on the beats, gybing on the run, line positioning before the gun — none of it programmed. Watch the gate split: the fleet usually discovers one side pays and herds there within ~30 generations, exactly like a real fleet converging on the favored side.
